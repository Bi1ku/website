Hobbies

I have a lot of hobbies that I enjoy to do during my free time! Some of these activites include robotics, programming, chess, and badminton. I will cover these topics in depth, so scroll down!

Robotics

I'm a member for the Stuyvesant robotics team, Stuy Fusion (FTC Team 479). We compete in competitions throughout NYC and the United States. It's been a great experience and I've learned a lot about engineering and lower-level hardware programming. The community is also great and I've made a lot of friends through the team. I would highly reccomend anyone to join! It's truly changed my life, and now I look forward to going to meeting, even if it's over the break.

Programming

I've been programming since the beginning of the pandemic. I've always loved the idea of technology and coding, but my first experience with it was through The Future Sphere's coding classes. I learned about Python, web development, and more. Using the skills learned from these classes, I've been able to join freelance web development teams, and so much more.

Chess

I love playing chess! I started playing in 6th grade, and I've been on and off the game since then. Though I haven't had the time to join Stuyvesant's Chess Club, I still play with my friends and family. I'm not the best, but I'm always trying to improve my game. I currently have a rating of 1300 on chess.com and play the Pirc Defense for black and London System for white.

Badminton

Badminton is my sport of choice. I love how simplistic the game is, but also the high skill gap that seperates the casuals from the professionals. During the summer, you can often find me playing this sport with my friends at the local park. Though, just like chess, I still have much to improve and work on. I've been thinking about trying out for Stuyvesant's Badminton Team, but we'll see.
