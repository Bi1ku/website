I'm Owen Shi, a current sophomore at Stuyvesant High School who's passionate about STEM. I was born in Queens, New York, on April 13th, 2008, and raised by a traditional Chinese household. At 5, I moved to Greensboro, North Carolina, where I went to elementary school (shoutout to Claxton Elementary!).

However, due to parental conflicts, I moved back to New York City during my 5th-grade summer, attending I.S. 5, Walter H. Crowley Middle School, a school in Queens. I was a bit of a loner, but I made a few friends. I was also a bit of a troublemaker, but a decent student. During the pandemic, which happened at the end of my 6th-grade year, I began to learn to program, which quite literally changed my life.

8th grade went smoothly and I went to Townsend Harris High School before transferring to Stuyvesant due to their superior STEM programs. My favorite classes are Annual Introduction to Computer Science and Physics right now.

I live in Woodside, Queens, with my mother and grandmother in a small house. My aunt and cousins live down the block, so it's really convenient. Though I do miss Greensboro's large houses and flourishing nature, I love New York's hustle culture and mentality which motivates me to work harder each and every day!

I'm also a huge fan of video games, and I play them in my free time. During my middle school years (especially over the pandemic), I remember playing for at least 4 hours per day (yikes!). These times are some of the most important in my life, as I've made many friends through these games. I was also able to learn a lot about teamwork, strategy, and communication. Though I've cut down on my gaming time due to school and robotics, I still play games occasionally and love their competitive aspects--I used to be in the top 0.5% in Call of Duty and Fortnite mobile using a 6-finger claw grip. Some of my favorite childhood games to play are:

  • Call Of Duty: Mobile
  • Fortnite Battle Royale
  • Valorant FPS Search & Destroy
  • Minecraft Sandbox and Sruvival

Games represent a huge part of my life, as they symbolize my competitve nature. I love the aspect of improving and honing one's skills.

Though, in hindsight, I do admit I was a little too addicted to them in my early youth, but it's no argument that they had a huge impact on my overall development. Do you have any favorite games and pastimes? I'd love to hear about them!
